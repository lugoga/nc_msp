Marine Spatial Planning

Published

December 5, 2025

Modified

December 30, 2025

Overview

This Marine Spatial Planning initiative focuses on developing sustainable coastal resource management strategies in Mkoani (Pemba Island), Zanzibar and Mkuranga districts within the Pwani region of mainland Tanzania. The consultancy integrates scientific research, stakeholder engagement, and participatory planning to support evidence-based marine governance.

Coastal Engagement in Mkoani-Pemba: Community stakeholders participating in marine spatial planning discussions

About the Consultancy

The Marine Spatial Planning Consultancy for Mkoani-Pemba and Mkuranga is a collaborative initiative led by the University of Dar es Salaam, School of Aquatic Sciences and Fisheries Technology (SOAF) in partnership with coastal communities and government authorities. The consultancy aims to create comprehensive spatial plans that balance conservation objectives with sustainable use of marine resources.

Study Sites: Mkoani and Mkuranga

Mkoani, Pemba (Unguja Region) and Mkuranga (Pwani Region) represent key coastal areas with significant fishing activities and diverse marine ecosystems. These sites were selected for:

  • Strategic importance for Indian Ocean fisheries
  • Presence of multiple livelihood communities dependent on marine resources
  • High biodiversity value and conservation potential
  • Need for improved governance and management frameworks

Consultancy Focus Areas

The initiative addresses key marine planning challenges through:

Focus Area Objective
Fisheries Management Develop sustainable fishing zones and seasonal closures based on resource dynamics
Biodiversity Conservation Identify and protect critical marine habitats and species
Coastal Communities Support livelihoods while ensuring resource sustainability
Climate Resilience Build adaptive management strategies for environmental changes
Governance Coordination Strengthen institutional frameworks for marine resource management

Spatial Planning Approach

This initiative employs a participatory and science-based approach to marine spatial planning, drawing on:

  • Community engagement: Inclusive consultation with fishers, boat owners, and coastal residents
  • Ecological data: Scientific surveys of fish populations, habitats, and marine biodiversity
  • Socioeconomic analysis: Assessment of livelihood patterns and economic dependencies on marine resources
  • Institutional coordination: Collaboration with district authorities and national management frameworks
  • Adaptive management: Iterative planning process with monitoring and refinement
